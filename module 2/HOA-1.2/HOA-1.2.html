Chicken Adobo: The Heart and Soul of Filipino Cuisine

Chicken Adobo is one of the most iconic dishes of the Philippines, celebrated for its rich, savory, and slightly tangy flavor. Slowly simmered in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns, this beloved dish reflects the Filipino tradition of simple ingredients transformed into something deeply comforting and unforgettable. More than just a meal, Chicken Adobo represents family, home, and the warmth of shared stories around the dining table.

Adobo is not just a dish; it is a way of life for Filipinos.

- Doreen G. Fernandez

Ingredients:

  • Chicken: 1lbs
  • Dried Bay Leaves: 3 pieces
  • Soy Sauce: 4 tablespoons
  • White Vinegar: 6 tablespoons
  • Cups of Water: 1 1/2
  • Cooking Oil: 3 tablespoon
  • Sugar: 1 teaspoon
  • Salt: 1/4 teaspoon
  • Whole Peppercorn: 1 teaspoon

Instructions:

  1. Combine chicken, soy sauce, and garlic in a large bowl. Mix well. Marinate the chicken for at least 1 hour. Note: the longer the time, the better.
  2. Heat a cooking pot. Pour cooking oil.
  3. When the oil is hot enough, pan-fry the marinated chicken for 2 minutes per side.
  4. Pour-in the remaining marinade, including garlic. Add water. Bring to a boil
  5. Add dried bay leaves and whole peppercorn. Simmer for 30 minutes or until the chicken gets tender
  6. Add vinegar. Stir and cook for 10 minutes.
  7. Put-in the sugar, and salt. Stir and turn the heat off.Serve hot. Share and Enjoy!
